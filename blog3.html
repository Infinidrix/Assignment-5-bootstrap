Ethiopian.

Oct 01, 2016

I smile as I read about it in textbooks, magazines and newspapers. My chest swells with pride as I see a stadium full of admirers looking down at my fellow brothers and sisters clothed by the Ethiopian flag on TV. When I hear about the towering obelisks of Axum, and the victory of Adwa, my heart ignites with joy and deep reverence to my ancestors. More or less such a feeling is common in all Ethiopians, which is the unifying force of our country.

Our history is the core of our entity. I like to imagine us as an Olympic swimming pool. For a casual observer it would seem that we are divided into our separate lanes, but if one dives in, they will see that our differences are only surface level and deep down we are all one and the same proud Ethiopian blood flows through our veins. As Ethiopians, I mean as a single identity, we are driven by our history. The victory of Adwa is a source of love for our own country. Maybe it is because our present life is not worth enough to fill us with an undying flame of patriotism, so we tend to look at our glorious past to absorb it into our being. We live on the light from the stars that surpass the limits of time. Our history matters to us, for our unity, for our patriotism and for our lives.

Then how can we be moving forward if we live in the past? How can we be keeping up with the pace if we hide in our ancestorâ€™s footprints? The answer for me is simple: as we uphold our history with such reverence, we desire to make history that others will write about. As for example, the Great Renaissance Dam is receiving such great support from all people regardless of religion, ethnic group and social status by encouraging them to make their mark in this historic event.

The reason we run is because we know our history, and if we want to run faster the first step should be to know our history better. We, as a nation, are defined by our history and we live to set a better definition, a higher standard, a greater unity, a stronger passion, a brighter future; we live to set our history.
